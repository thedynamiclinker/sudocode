```
אל (El)
	(Hebrew) god.
	Not necessarily the god of any particular faith.
	Rather, the connotations are similar to the English
	word "god", which can be used to refer to deities
	of any religion, independent of the speaker's
	individual beliefs or lack thereof.


שדי (Shadday)

Etymology 1

	Unknown. Possibly from שָׁד (shad, “breast”) +‎ ־ַי (-áy),
	indicating a connection between God and nourishment.

Etymology 2

	שֵׁד (shéd, “demon”) +‎ ־ִי (-í)

	שֵׁדִי • (shedí)
	demonic, mischievous, deceitful, malicious
	
Proper noun

	שַׁדי (shaddáy)
	Epithet for God, often translated as "Almighty"
```
