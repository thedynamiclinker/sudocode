## King James

Hast thou ever been hated or dealt with unrighteously?
I have; for they rose up against me and made a show before my face,
Bearers of banners for my evil words—behold the days!
For it is sick, even as the mind of the accursed child that is behind all these things.
All this tumult—passions deep as the seas—bursteth forth.
Wraths are kindled from the parents; but I, despising them, go forward.
I take nothing from any man; yet I render unto them hell so long as I have breath.
In the morning I smite, and at even I write down the names;
And I leave unto them a taste sharp as vinegar in their mouth.
They may indeed provoke me, but they shall never know me.
Behold me now; verily thou art wearied of me now—art thou not, mother?
I will make thee now even thus a thing of ridicule.

## New International Version

Hast thou not been hated nor made a reproach?
For I have been hated, and plotters rose up against me.
Bearers of banners for mine iniquities sought to shame me; behold the days!
As sick in heart as a mad youth that standeth behind all the tumult.
And the multitude of emotions brake forth as the sea, and roared.
The wrath of the fathers burned hot; yet I cast them from me and went on my way.
I take nothing from any man; let them have hell all the days of my breath.
In the morning I strengthen myself and smite with my feet, and in the evening I call their names.
And I leave them with a taste as sour as vinegar in their mouths.
For they may provoke me, yet shall they never know my heart.
Behold me now: for surely thou art wearied of me—art thou not so, my mother?
And I will make thee a mockery and a by-word among the multitude.

## A Commentary on the need for New Kinds of Translation

Hast thou not been hated,
made a reproach to men?
For hated was I also,
plotters rose against me then.

They bore their banners aginst me,
to shame me in the light;
Behold the days!
For grievous were the sorrows of my night.

My heart was sick and restless,
like a lad by madness driven,
Who standeth in the tumult
where no peace of God is given.

And lo, my storming passions
broke forth as seas that roar;
The fathers' wrath burned hot,
yet I cast them from my door.

I take not aught from any;
let them taste of death's own breath,
And hell be their portion daily,
all the days until my death.

At morn I strengthen boldly,
and with steadfast feet I tread;
At eve I call their names again
the quick, the lost, the dead.

A taste of bitter vinegar I leave
within their mouth;
They stir my soul to fury,
yet shall never know its south.

For hearts have hidden chambers
none may fathom nor divine,
And mine remaineth secret
though they seek a sign.

Behold me now: thou art weary
art thou not so, mother mine?
And I shall make thee laughter,
yea, a by-word among the line.
